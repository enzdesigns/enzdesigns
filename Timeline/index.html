The right notification at the right time - example use case

11:00AM

Dan Foreman takes the train to SF. While on the train he captures his impression from the meeting earlier that day: "Meeting with John went well".

11:05AM

Few minutes later, Dan remembers that since the meeting took place over breakfast, he should expense it. He captures "Met with John for breakfast and it was $30".

12:15PM

As he grabs lunch, Dan looks at the notifications on his phone and notices two new items. His earlier note "Meeting with John went well" shows a system generated suggestion to log a Call Report. Dan Accepts the suggestion, which creates the report. With one tap he is done.

Next he opens the second notification. This one is a reminder about his breakfast expense. The system suggests an expense form that is automatically filled with content, based on the capture and the time and date it was entered. Dan checks that all the information is correct and taps on "Accept" to generate the report.
Read more >

What did you just see?

  • The framework identifies the string of text as a "Capture Event" and invokes the CHASER NLU component.

  • Identification of John as John Laweson the client was done using the Relationship Network.

  • The user intent to log an expense was identified using NLP and NLU.

  • The combination of keywords like 'breakfast' and 'John' who is a client, helped define the intent.

  • NLP, Location and Time and Relationship Network were all used to populate the fields in the expense form.

12:30PM

Taking a quickly look at his phone Dan notices a notification about his upcoming meeting in 15 minutes. He clears up his tray and quickly walks toward the meeting location.

12:50PM

As he arrives at the meeting location he takes one last look at his phone, and sees a new SR alert from the customers he is about to meet. The SR states that the server is down and is causing a real issue. Dan quickly calls the support contact on the account to get an update on the issue and how it is being addressed.
Read more >

What did you just see?

  • When the user logs into the mobile app, the framework identifies the "login event" and invokes the Relationship Network to identify the most important task for the user.

  • The SR alert is being pushed to the user using Logic Rules that identify the urgency level of the SR based on time, location, and upcoming activities.

  • Logic Rules also helped identify that the SR alert is more important than the Upcoming Meeting notification and therefore placed it at the top of the screen.

1:00PM

Dan goes into the meeting fully prepared to talk about the issue, reassuring the customer that fixing this problem is a high priority for his business, that he has already contacted the support team, and that they are cranking through it. He promises to get back to them with an ETA within two hours: "Your business is important to us, and we are doing everything we can to fix this issue asap."

2:05PM

Dan's meeting went well. Heading back to the train station, he captures: "Meeting regarding Green Server was good. Win percentage higher."

3:10PM

Dan takes a quick look at his capture summarizing his 1pm meeting and the system suggestions to create a Call Report and update the Win Percentage for the Green Server opportunity. He wants to put a bit more thought into it, as well as check the status of the SR. He will deal with it later once back in the office.

4:30PM

Back in the office, Dan uses his laptop to log into his Oracle applications. He sees few recommended actions on the banner view, and clicks on the bell icon to read notifications. His earlier capture is there reminding him about the outcome of the meeting he had in SF. Now that he has a bit more time, he can take the required steps to follow up on open items.
